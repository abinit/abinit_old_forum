Hi

It is normal that you don't have the same number of kpoints in the ground state and in the DFPT calculation. In the ground state calculation, you have kptopt=1 whereas in the DFPT calculation, kptopt=2. If you want to remove this warning, just put kptopt1=2 and it will be ok. But it's just a warning, so you can leave it be. And since you have 5 times more kpoints in the DFPT calculation, bandtot=400 (5*80).

As for the negative phonon frequency, it can stem from the fact that the pseudopotentials that you are using break the acoustic sum rule. To check this, use anaddb (as explained in the tutorial) in order to force the acoustic sum rule. Your phonon frequencies should then be all positive.

Boris

Anaddb is supposed to give you the best results. Plus, it is consistent that the three acoustic modes are zero at the gamma point. This is always the case, except when the acoustic sum rule is broken.

So I would trust anaddb.
