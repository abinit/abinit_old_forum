Hello, several points:
- for your make failing in 98_main, I suppose that you configure ABINIT first without --disable-etsf-io and make, then, you reconfigure again, with it and recompile. This is broken for Fortran because of lack of dependency tracking.
- if you don't disable etsf_io, then there will be some trouble for V_Sim to build the library and you should then pass by hand ETSF_IO also to the linker.

So for it to work, you should:
- use the ability of the autotools to build out of the source tree;
- disable ETSF_IO during the build used later by V_Sim.

Step by step:
- download a fresh 6.0.4 version;
- create two directories 'build-prod' and 'build-lib' inside it;
- in build-prod, run '../configure FC=mpif90 FCFLAGS="top optimization flags" --enable-mpi ...' and make;
- in build-lib, run '../configure --disable-etsf-io --enable-bindings --prefix=$HOME/usr' and make and make install;
- then in V_Sim directory, configure with --with-abinit=$HOME/usr.

Thanks to the work of Yann Pouillon, the #define stuff to do in the C sources is not necessary anymore from 6.3.0 and I rework the stuff to avoid also the --disable-etsf-io.
